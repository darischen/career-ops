**Fit: Minor** (4.0/5)

1. **Competitive hiring.** Blue Origin is prestigious (aerospace + Bezos). You're competing against MIT, Carnegie, Stanford EE grads. Your CV is strong, but "new grad computer engineering" is common.

2. **Relocation required.** Seattle is your opposite coast (San Diego to Seattle, ~2,200 miles). You said you'd relocate, but this is a real move. However, Seattle is a tech hub with good quality of life.

3. **Aerospace software ≠ consumer software.** The pace is different. You might ship code every 3-6 months, not weekly. It's safer, but less "iteration velocity" than PatentIQ.

4. **Security clearance process.** Aerospace work requires clearance. Expect 6+ months of vetting before you start classified work. Not a blocker, but bureaucratic.

5. **Rotation program structure.** The New Graduate Rotation is intentional development, but it also means you're moved every 4 months. Some people love this; some find it disruptive. You're adaptable, but worth considering.

**None are dealbreakers, but they're real.**
