### Story 2: Advocated for Technical Approach & Won (PatentIQ)

**Situation:** PatentIQ MVP design decision. PMs proposed MongoDB + Pinecone (managed vector DB) or Weaviate.

**Task:** Recommend database architecture for semantic patent search.

**Action:**
- Analyzed trade-offs: Pinecone costs $$ for MVP; Postgres + pgvector is open-source + already running

**Reflection:** "Technical judgment isn't about knowing all frameworks. It's about understanding trade-offs (cost, velocity, risk) and making the call that fits the stage we're at."

### Story 5: Domain-Specific Technical Depth (PatentIQ semantic search)

**Situation:** Build hybrid semantic search engine for USPTO patent database (~8M patents).

**Task:** Design search that combines full-text (Postgres) + semantic similarity (embeddings) for accurate patent retrieval.

**Action:**
- Evaluated approach: BM25 ranking + pgvector cosine similarity
- Implemented two-stage pipeline: keyword search first (fast), then semantic re-rank for top results
- Optimized embedding strategy: OpenAI text-embedding-3-small, chunk patents by section

**Result:** Semantic search outperformed full-text-only by 40% on relevance; 80ms query latency on 8M dataset.

**Reflection:** "The most interesting engineering is at the seam between your data and your models. Understanding both sides (SQL optimization + embedding spaces) beats knowing one deeply."
