## Fit Assessment

### Why This Works

1. **RF/signal processing:** Your HB100 phased array radar project is directly relevant. This is a strong proof point.
2. **Embedded systems:** TriCore9, ESP32, Raspberry Pi experience transfers to DoD signal processing.
3. **DSP background:** FFT, Butterworth filters, Kalman filtering (HB100) matches the modeling/simulation work.

### Why It's Problematic

1. **Security clearance:** Obtaining a secret or top-secret clearance takes 3–6 months minimum. This delays start date significantly.
2. **Career trajectory:** DoD contractor work doesn't translate to commercial tech. You'll be specialized in defense-specific systems.
3. **Speed of innovation:** Defense contracting moves slowly. You thrive in fast iteration (PatentIQ, Groundwork).
4. **Market expertise:** Defense doesn't build resume value for startups or Big Tech AI roles.
5. **Location lock:** Beavercreek, Ohio. You're flexible on relocation, but this ties you to one place for a clearance.

## Red Flags & Concerns

1. **Clearance process:** Months of background investigation. Can't start full work until cleared.
2. **Scope creep:** Once you're in DoD, you're in a specialized ecosystem. Hard to exit to commercial tech without retraining.
3. **Export control:** Anything you learn is ITAR/EAR controlled. Can't discuss with non-cleared colleagues.
4. **Culture mismatch:** DoD is methodical, process-heavy. You prefer pragmatism and shipping fast.
