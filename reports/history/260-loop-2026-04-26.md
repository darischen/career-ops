Choose based on your preference. Loop is better if you want to be thrown into the deep end and learn fast. Replit is better if you want sustainable growth and product exposure.

**Archetype fit:**
Full-Stack Software Engineer (primary). Secondary: AI/ML Engineer (LLM systems). Loop's focus on "production AI systems" and "LLM agents" means your RedShift adversarial testing and Claude integration work directly applies.

**Compensation:**
$150K base (SF) is top-tier new grad compensation. Market comparison:
- Google/Meta new grad: $120K-$150K base
- Loop: $150K (at top end)

No equity amount disclosed, but typical for Series B is 0.05-0.1% (worth $500K-$1M depending on future exit).

**Culture & Growth:**
Loop's new grad program is explicitly designed to "compress 3 years into 1 year." This is high-risk, high-reward:
- Risk: Burnout, imposter syndrome, attrition
- Reward: Accelerated growth, rapid skill acquisition, strong network (peers, executives)

The rotation structure (Platform, Core Product, Commercial, Special Projects) is excellent for breadth. "Special Projects" with CEO/CTO is rare and valuable.

**Why apply despite on-site requirement:**
The program quality, compensation, and growth trajectory are top-tier. If you're willing to relocate (your profile says yes), SF for one year is a reasonable investment. Loop's Series B stage means rapid growth and potential for promotion to Senior SWE by Year 2.

**Risk assessment:**
The "3 years into 1" framing could mean: (a) great mentorship and fast feedback, or (b) burnout and high expectations. Your overcommitment tendency suggests you might thrive in this environment, but watch for warning signs (excessive hours, no mentorship, contradictory feedback).
