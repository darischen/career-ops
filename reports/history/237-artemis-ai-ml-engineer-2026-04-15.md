## F. Interview Prep

**Technical focus (expect rigorous interviews):**

1. **PatentIQ semantic search architecture.**
   - Why OpenAI embeddings over open-source or fine-tuned alternatives?
   - How did you design the hybrid ranking pipeline (embeddings + traditional search)?
   - How would you scale PatentIQ's approach to handle billions of documents (not millions)?
   - Query latency optimization techniques.
   - Real-time indexing and updates.

2. **FlipperZillow GPU pipeline.**
   - Multi-stage inference orchestration (DepthAnythingV2 → DFormerV2 → SAM3D).
   - Model selection rationale. Why those models over alternatives?
   - Inference latency optimization. How did you reduce end-to-end latency?
   - Why SAM3D failed on ROCm. Debugging process. Technical decision to pivot.
   - GPU resource allocation and scheduling.

3. **Mini-Stockfish data pipeline (most relevant for Artemis).**
   - How did you design a pipeline to train on 316M examples?
   - Memory-mapped files, async batch loading, streaming data architecture.
   - How would you scale this to handle exabyte-scale telemetry data (Artemis context)?
   - Trade-offs: batch size, training time, convergence, accuracy.
   - Monitoring and alerting during training.

4. **LLM systems thinking.**
   - How would you represent complex structured security telemetry to an LLM?
   - Prompting vs. fine-tuning trade-offs. When would you choose each?
   - Agentic workflows. Have you built systems where LLMs make decisions/take actions?
   - Model selection. How do you choose between proprietary (GPT-4) vs. open-source (Llama) for a production system?
   - Cost optimization at scale. How do you reduce inference costs when processing exabytes of data?

5. **Security domain questions (expect to learn on the job).**
   - What's a cyber threat? (Basic understanding expected; depth not required yet.)
   - How would you approach building an ML system to detect anomalous behavior in corporate telemetry?
   - What's the difference between rule-based detection and learned detection?

**Behavioral focus:**
1. **Learning velocity.** FlipperZillow: learned 5 new frameworks in 24 hours. Artemis values this for rapid domain onboarding.
2. **Production mindset.** PatentIQ shipped with real users. You understand trade-offs between accuracy and shipping speed.
3. **Team leadership.** PatentIQ's 14-person team. You coordinated across data scientist, backend engineer, frontend developer. Artemis values cross-functional collaboration.
4. **Pragmatism under pressure.** SAM3D pivot story: made hard decision to cut scope and ship. Artemis culture emphasizes "move fast with high quality."

**Likely questions:**
- Walk me through your most complex ML system end-to-end.
- Describe a time you had to debug a production ML issue.
- How would you approach learning a new domain (cybersecurity) while shipping?
- Tell me about a time you made a major technical decision (model selection, architecture, etc.) and how you justified it.
- Why Artemis? Why AI/ML engineering?
- How do you think about LLMs as a tool for solving problems?
