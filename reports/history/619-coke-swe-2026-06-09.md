## Block F — Interview Prep

**Situation 1: Shipped Under Ambiguity (PatentIQ)**

**Situation:** Product undefined. PMs wanted AI patent search + drafting assistant. No clear spec. 14-member team, 6-week deadline.

**Task:** Own backend architecture when requirements were vague and shifting.

**Action:**
- Interviewed PMs to surface core use case: "Find relevant patents, extract claims, draft responses quickly."
- Proposed architecture: semantic search (embeddings) + ranking + drafting template. Simpler than full generation.
- Convinced team PostgreSQL + pgvector sufficient for MVP (avoid Pinecone complexity).
- Iterated with PMs weekly. Cut nice-to-haves (full LLM drafting → template-based drafting).
- Shipped MVP in 6 weeks.

**Result:** Users could search 1M+ patents. Drafting assistant reduced time-to-response by 30% (estimate from PMs).

**Reflection:** Product thinking is iterative. You don't need perfect spec upfront; you need clarity on the core value, then iterate.

**Situation 2: Technical Decision-Making Under Pressure (PatentIQ)**

**Situation:** Data scientist + backend engineer + PMs all proposed different database solutions (MongoDB, Pinecone, Weaviate).

**Task:** Choose the path forward without overriding team or dictating.

**Action:**
- Asked data scientist and backend engineer to research independently. Gave them 2 days.
- Shared my reasoning: "We already run Postgres. pgvector is free, open-source. MVP doesn't need hosted vector DB yet."

- Got buy-in without dictatorship. Faster iteration, lower cost.

**Result:** Technical decision made collaboratively. Team owned it.

**Reflection:** Good technical leaders persuade with data, not authority. Especially as new grad, demonstrate judgment, not ego.

**Situation 3: Learned Fast, Adapted Scope (FlipperZillow)**

**Situation:** 24-hour hackathon. No prior experience with GPU infrastructure, modern ML frameworks, Vision Pro development.
