## Detailed Analysis

**Match with CV:**
Eventual explicitly needs Python + distributed systems knowledge. Your PatentIQ architecture (hybrid semantic search, architecture decisions, performance optimization) demonstrates systems thinking. Your FlipperZillow GPU pipeline work shows you can orchestrate complex systems under time pressure.

The Daft engine focuses on "coordinating with external APIs, managing GPU clusters, and handling failures." Your experience with:
- Semantic search architecture (complex data structures)
- Performance optimization (77.2% latency reduction in chess engine, 90% API latency reduction in Groundwork)
- Real-time systems (inventory synchronization)

All apply directly.

**Archetype fit:**
This is infrastructure/platform engineering (secondary to your full-stack preference). You'll be building systems that power AI applications, not the applications themselves. If you enjoy the abstraction and want to master distributed systems, this is excellent. If you prefer building user-facing features, skip this.

**Compensation:**
$120K-$150K is at the lower end of the new grad market. However:
- Y Combinator backing means potential for growth (acquisition or IPO)
- Equity at Series B can be 0.1-0.3%, worth $100K-$500K at exit
- Founders with track record (Databricks exit was valuable, Perplexity is hot)

Total comp Year 1: probably $150K-$180K with equity, but equity is riskier than cash at public companies.

**Why apply despite lower comp:**
1. Elite founder team (you'll learn from the best)
2. Open-source leadership position (Daft is public, builds resume)
3. Systems thinking expertise (generalizable to any company)
4. Y Combinator network (valuable for future opportunities)
5. Fallback if Replit/Loop/Palantir don't work out
