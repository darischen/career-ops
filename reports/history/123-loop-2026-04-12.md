### Recommended Case Study for Presentation

**PatentIQ: End-to-End Data Infrastructure**

**Why:** Loop builds a "supply chain data platform." PatentIQ is also a data platform (patents instead of logistics). Perfect proxy.

**What to present (10-15 min):**
1. **Problem:** Founders can't afford patent counsel. Keyword search on USPTO is useless (patents are dense, full of jargon).
2. **Data layer:** 10M+ patents. How do you make them queryable? Answer: semantic embeddings + hybrid ranking.
3. **Architecture decisions:**
   - OpenAI text-embeddings (off-the-shelf, works well)
   - PostgreSQL + pgvector (scalable, no managed service dependency)
   - Hybrid ranking: 70% semantic, 30% keyword (handles edge cases)
4. **Infrastructure wins:**
   - Recursive SQL query builder with Zod validation (safe, composable)
   - LRU cache for embeddings (avoid recomputing)
   - FastAPI backend with structured logging
5. **Results:** <2s latency on 10M patent search, 15% precision improvement over keyword-only.
6. **Team context:** Led 14-person team through PMA. Weekly design reviews. Shipped MVP in 4 months.

**Loop will ask:** "How would you approach structuring supply chain data from PDFs and emails?"

**Your answer:** "Similar approach: ETL pipeline (extract unstructured data from PDFs → parse into structured records → store in normalized schema), semantic embeddings for fuzzy matching (shipment dates, vendor names, POs), hybrid ranking for relevance. The difference is domain: instead of patent keywords, I'm matching logistics entities (shipper, receiver, tracking number). The infrastructure pattern is the same."
