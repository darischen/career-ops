**Red Flags:**

1. **Clearance requirement is a blocker** — "US Citizenship with interim Secret or Secret clearance" is standard for federal contractors. As a new grad US Citizen, you're eligible for interim Secret sponsorship, but it adds 2-4 week delay to start date and background check complexity. Verify they sponsor clearances before applying.

2. **No compensation listed** — Lack of transparency about pay. Federal contractor roles are often underpaid relative to commercial software.

3. **Federal contracting culture** — Slower iteration, more process (change control boards, compliance reviews), security-focused rather than feature-focused. Your superpower is rapid domain switching and shipping under pressure. Federal work is the opposite: slow, deliberate, heavily regulated.

4. **Java / Spring Boot ramp** — Not insurmountable, but adds 2-4 weeks of learning. If they need you shipping on day 1, this becomes a friction point.

5. **DevSecOps emphasis** — Security-hardened systems are slower to iterate on. Your preference is pragmatism + fast shipping (e.g., "cut SAM3D, ship working product").

**Wins:**

1. **Remote** — Full remote is your preference.

2. **Stable work** — Federal contractor roles are stable, not going anywhere. Good if you want predictability.

3. **Full-stack match** — React expertise is strong. PostgreSQL experience is deep. You'll contribute quickly on the frontend.

4. **Tech stack is modern** — React, Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Docker, Jenkins are all industry-standard and valuable skills. Not legacy tech (no COBOL, Swing, etc.).

5. **Learning opportunity** — Java / Spring Boot / DevSecOps are transferable skills. After this role, you could move to commercial tech easily.
