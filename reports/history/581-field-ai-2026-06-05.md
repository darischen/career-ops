### Story 5: Shipped Under Hardware Constraints

**Situation:** HB100 Phased Array Microwave Radar (analog + embedded + DSP)  
**Task:** Build working direction-finding system with limited hardware (op-amp noise, local oscillator drift, tight ADC resolution)  
**Action:**
- Designed dual-stage op-amp cascade (MCP6002) for 1092× gain on microvolt IF signals
- Implemented 4th-Order Butterworth filtering + FFT for frequency domain analysis
- Wrote Extended Kalman Filter for 2D spatial tracking despite phase-coherent noise
- When phase-coherence failed, pivoted to amplitude monopulse (different algorithm, no lost time)

**Result:** Accurate direction estimation and velocity tracking despite hardware limitations.

**Reflection:** Real hardware has constraints. You can't always get perfect signals. The key is understanding where problems originate, choosing algorithms that work *with* the constraints, and shipping something reliable.

**Why it matters for FieldAI:** Field robotics means operating in unstructured, real-world environments. Sensors fail, communication drops, compute is limited. You need to think about reliability and graceful degradation, not just clean-room assumptions.
