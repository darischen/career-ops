## Summary

Westinghouse is hiring for the "Catalyst Program"—a 2-year rotational leadership development program for new grads in AI, digital innovation, and nuclear energy. You'd rotate across multiple departments (AI Delivery, APX Product Operations, Outage Services), develop leadership, then transition to a permanent role. This is more structured than typical entry-level, but location lock, rotational friction, and defense/nuclear focus make it a moderate fit at best.

### E. Red Flags

**Score: -0.8**

1. **2-year program commitment:** Catalyst is designed for long-term retention. You'll be pigeon-holed into Westinghouse culture. If you want to leave after 2 years, you'll have "program dropout" stigma.
2. **Location lock:** "Local Pittsburgh candidates only" = relocation required, limited flexibility. You're in San Diego.
3. **Rotational dilution:** 6 months in AI Delivery, 6 months in operations, 6 months in outage services. You won't achieve depth in any domain. Compare to Glean: 1 year of focused ML engineering.
4. **Nuclear domain risk:** If you leave Westinghouse after 2 years, "2 years in nuclear energy" on your resume is niche. Tech companies will see you as "domain-specific," not "modern AI engineer."
5. **Post-rotation placement:** JD says "Upon successful completion... participant will transition into a permanent position on the AI & Innovation Digital Technologies team." This is a promise, not a guarantee. If they don't have a role, what happens?

## Interview Strategy

If you interview, ask:
1. "What's the typical placement process after Catalyst? What if AI Delivery doesn't have an opening?"
2. "Do rotations require relocation, or is Cranberry the home base?"
3. "How many Catalyst alumni have left Westinghouse within 3 years? What's the post-program retention?"

These questions signal you're considering commitment length, not just taking the role.
