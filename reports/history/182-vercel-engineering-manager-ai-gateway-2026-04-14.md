  *You have no team.*
  
- "How do you evaluate engineer performance?"  
  *You've never conducted a performance review.*
  
- "Describe your approach to hiring"  
  *You've never interviewed candidates.*

**These aren't skills you can fake or learn on the job in an interview.** A hiring manager at Vercel (managing a critical AI infrastructure team) will terminate the interview after one or two answers.

### The Founder/Accelerator Advantage (Doesn't Apply Here)

Your PatentIQ experience showed **leadership of a 14-member team** (designers, PMs, engineers). This is genuinely impressive and would be **excellent context for a mid-level IC role or future PM exploration.** But it's **not the same as engineering management:**

- You were an **architect + primary contributor** (still hands-on IC work)
- You did **not hire, fire, performance-manage, or set compensation** for that team
- You did **not own team velocity, career development, or headcount planning**

**Don't claim this is management experience.** Hiring managers at top-tier companies know the difference, and misrepresenting it signals dishonesty.
