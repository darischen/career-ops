Ciena explicitly lists "2026 New Grads" in the title. This role targets students graduating this spring/summer. Your profile (TriCore9 + HB100 + Mini-Stockfish) demonstrates embedded systems expertise that many new grads lack.

1. **Hardware-software integration.** TriCore9 shows you understand CPU architecture, pipelining, hazard detection. HB100 shows you can condition analog signals, write firmware, process data in real-time. Ciena builds optical equipment; your hardware intuition is directly applicable.
2. **Performance optimization.** Chess engine (77.2% latency reduction), HB100 signal processing (Kalman filtering, FFT optimization), TriCore9 (pipeline hazard mitigation). Ciena values low-latency systems for networking.
3. **Embedded firmware.** ESP32-S3 firmware (interrupt handling, DMA, USB-CDC protocol). This is closer to what Ciena does than web development. You have relevant hands-on experience.
4. **Real-time systems thinking.** HB100 data collection @ 10kHz in real-time. You understand jitter, latency budgets, and responsive systems. Networking equipment must operate in real-time.

**Positioning:**

"Ciena builds high-speed optical networking platforms. I'm comfortable learning new domains—I've shifted from FPGA to AI to signal processing to full-stack web. Optical networking is another domain I can master.

I value the Ciena culture description: people-first, pragmatic approach, flexible work. I'm eager to grow technically and contribute to systems that power global communications."

2. **On-site in Atlanta:** You're willing to relocate anywhere in the US. Georgia is affordable, decent tech community (growing). No red flag here.
