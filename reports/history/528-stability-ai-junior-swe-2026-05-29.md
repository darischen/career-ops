## Interview Prep

### Story 1: Full-Stack Architecture Under Complexity

**Q: Tell me about a time you had to make an architectural decision with incomplete information.**

**A:** "At PatentIQ, the team proposed three database options: MongoDB, Pinecone, or Weaviate for semantic search. All three would have worked, but I advocated for PostgreSQL + pgvector instead.

Why? Postgres was already running, pgvector is free and open-source, and the MVP didn't need hosted vector DB yet. More importantly, staying in Postgres let us iterate on schema without vendor lock-in.

I didn't just assert this—I asked the data scientist and backend engineer to research it independently. They came back agreeing within days after checking benchmarks. The decision saved cost and accelerated shipping. That's what I value in engineering: data-driven judgment, not overriding people."

**Why it works:** Shows architectural thinking, consensus-building, not dictatorship.

---

### Story 2: Performance Optimization

**Q: Walk me through a time you optimized a system under constraint.**

**A:** "At Groundwork Books, the Square API was a bottleneck. Real-time inventory updates were hitting their rate limits during peak traffic.

I built a two-tier caching system: Redis for backend (5-minute TTL, reducing Square hits by 90%), plus client-side request coalescing (batching 50+ concurrent lookups into one API call with 2-minute cache).

Result: sub-200ms p99 latency, even during Black Friday-level traffic. The key was understanding the bottleneck—not just throwing cache everywhere, but measuring it first."

**Why it works:** Shows systems thinking, measurement-driven approach, not premature optimization.

---

### Story 3: Learning Velocity

**Q: Tell me about a time you had to learn something outside your expertise quickly.**

**A:** "FlipperZillow (24-hour hackathon): I had to build an AI house tour platform. I'd never used ROCm, DepthAnythingV2, DFormerV2, or SAM3D before.

I spent 4 hours learning the ecosystem, set up an AMD cloud environment, integrated all five models into a pipeline. When SAM3D broke on ROCm (dependency conflict), I didn't waste time debugging—I pivoted to Nvidia cloud with CUDA, and we shipped.

The lesson: shipping a working product with 80% of your original features is better than a perfect design that doesn't exist. That pragmatism is what I bring to a team."

**Why it works:** Shows learning velocity, pragmatism, shipping mindset.
