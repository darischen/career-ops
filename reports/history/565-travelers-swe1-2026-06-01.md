2. **Fortune 500 bureaucracy.** Slower iteration cycles than startups. Code review processes, deployment gates, compliance. You value "fast iteration" — this isn't GitHub-speed. But it's a tradeoff for stability and learning.
3. **C# requirement.** You've never used it. But it's learnable in 2–3 weeks, and Travelers will pair you with senior engineers during onboarding.
4. **Legacy code.** Insurance systems often have decades of legacy COBOL/mainframe code. **Deal-breaker risk:** If you're assigned to COBOL (unlikely for a new grad on a modern team), this violates your deal-breaker policy. Mitigate by asking during interview: "What % of codebase is legacy vs. modern?" If they say "mostly COBOL," decline.
